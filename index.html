Summer Holiday

Siargao

There are numerous famous lagoons in the Philippines with picture-perfect blue seas and towering cliffsides, but Sugba Lagoon on the island of Siargao is one of the most breathtaking. The lagoon is ideal for relaxing and taking in the natural surroundings, but there are also lots of activities available if you want a more active day.

Taal Volcano

One of the most active volcanoes in the nation is the Taal Volcano on the island of Luzon. Since it is located in the middle of Taal Lake, the journey there alone is an adventure. It takes less than an hour to drive from Manila to the city of Talisay, where you will board a boat to get to the crater. After taking a boat across Taal Lake, you'll climb to the crater's rim.

Pagsanjan Falls

Admiring one of the Philippines' most famous waterfalls is exciting, but paddling across it in a dugout canoe is even more thrilling. The Pagsanjan Gorge National Park includes the waterfall at Pagsanjan Falls. You may take day trips from Manila to experience this thrilling outdoor activity. Once you've gotten acclimated to the leisurely ride, get ready for the big event as you proceed downstream at a quick clip through enormous rocks and churning water for what the locals call "shooting the rapids."

Fort Santiago

You may easily explore Manila on foot or join a sightseeing tour guide to view the city's oldest neighborhood, Intramuros, or walled city, where some of the most notable tourist attractions, significant landmarks, and monuments are located. You'll be able to appreciate Manila more as a result of this. Monuments at Rizal Park and Fort Santiago, as well as the well-known San Agustin Church, all had a big impact on Manila and are best explained by a guide.

Cebu

Cebu is the top tourism destination in the nation, and for good reason. It offers the greatest variety of activities and attractions, which is unmatched. Cebu is the "Jack of all Trades" of Philippine tourism destinations.

Vigan

The streets of Calle Crisologo in Vigan City, which is located in the Ilocos province, are a great spot to visit with a fantastic pre-colonial flair. Compared to other Philippine cities, it is distinctive. The brick lanes and conventional horse-and-carriage method of transportation bring to mind the region's heyday as a 16th-century international commerce centre.
